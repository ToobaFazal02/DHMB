EXECUTIVE SUMMARY & PROBLEM STATEMENT 1.1 Overview The Digital Handicraft Marketplace for Balochistan (DHMB) is an AI-enabled, data-driven digital commerce platform designed to economically empower artisans across Balochistan Province by connecting them directly with national and international buyers. The platform eliminates exploitative intermediaries, enables global digital access through multilingual tools, and leverages big data analytics to forecast demand, optimize pricing strategies, and guide informed production decisions. DHMB integrates a no-code marketplace interface, scalable cloud data infrastructure, and machine learning analytics to transform traditional handicraft businesses into globally competitive digital enterprises. By providing direct market access, the platform addresses systemic economic marginalization while preserving cultural heritage through sustainable digital commerce. 1.2 Problem Domain Analysis 1.2.1 Socioeconomic Context Balochistan possesses rich handicraft traditions encompassing embroidery, textiles, gemstones, woodcraft, and cultural artifacts. Despite this cultural wealth, artisans face severe structural economic barriers: Exploitative Intermediaries: Middlemen capture 5070% of product revenue, leaving artisans with minimal profit margins Digital Literacy Deficit: Limited technological proficiency restricts online market participation Linguistic Barriers: Language constraints prevent access to global customer bases Geographic Isolation: Rural artisan communities lack exposure to national and international buyers Absence of Market Intelligence: No structured demand forecasting or pricing optimization systems exist Gender Economic Disparity: Women constitute approximately 70% of the handicraft workforce yet lack direct income channels and financial autonomy Despite significant export potential, artisans remain systematically disconnected from modern e-commerce ecosystems, resulting in persistent economic vulnerability. 1.2.2 Current State Assessment Existing market infrastructure exhibits critical operational deficiencies: Fragmented Distribution Channels: Artisans are confined to local markets with no unified online platform for national or international reach.

DATA ARCHITECTURE (LAKEHOUSE MODEL) The DHMB platform implements a medallion architecture pattern for data management, organizing information into Bronze, Silver, and Gold tiers to progressively refine data quality while maintaining complete audit trails and data lineage. 3.1 Bronze Layer Raw Data Purpose: Immutable capture of all marketplace events and user interactions in their original format. Data Sources: Product view events and search queries User clickstream behavior and navigation patterns Transaction records and payment confirmations Inventory updates and stock modifications User activity logs and session data Storage Characteristics: Format: JSON documents preserving original event structure Retention Period: 24 months for regulatory compliance and historical analysis Write Pattern: Append-only, no updates or deletions Access Pattern: Batch processing for ETL pipelines 3.2 Silver Layer Cleaned Data Purpose: Validated, standardized, and enriched datasets ready for analytical processing. Data Transformations: Duplicate record removal using composite key matching Missing value imputation via statistical methods Currency normalization to PKR base currency Product category standardization using controlled vocabularies Timestamp harmonization to UTC Data Quality Metrics: Completeness: >95% of required fields populated Accuracy: >98% validation success rate Consistency: Referential integrity enforcement across related entities 3.3 Gold Layer Analytics Ready Purpose: Pre-aggregated, business-intelligence-optimized datasets for dashboard visualization and machine learning model training.

BIG DATA ANALYTICS ENGINE The analytics engine employs machine learning algorithms and statistical models to generate actionable insights for demand forecasting, dynamic pricing, and customer segmentation. 4.1 Demand Forecasting Model Methodology: Time-series forecasting using supervised learning algorithms to predict future product demand at category and geographic levels. Predictive Formula: Demand Score = (Search Volume × Conversion Rate × Seasonal Factor) + Trend Component Model Specifications: Algorithm: Gradient Boosted Decision Trees (XGBoost) Features: Historical sales, search patterns, seasonal indicators, external economic data Training Data: 18 months of historical transaction records Retraining Frequency: Weekly recalibration to capture evolving market dynamics Performance Target: 75% forecast accuracy (measured by MAPE) 4.2 Price Optimization Model Methodology: Statistical analysis of historical sales performance to identify optimal price points that maximize conversion rates while maintaining competitive positioning. Optimization Formula: Dynamic Pricing Mechanism: Real-time adjustment based on conversion rate feedback Competitor price monitoring and benchmarking Demand elasticity calculation for price sensitivity analysis 4.3 Buyer Segmentation Methodology: Unsupervised clustering using K-Means algorithm to identify distinct customer behavioral segments. Segmentation Features: Purchase frequency and recency (RFM analysis) Geographic location and regional preferences Product category affinity and cross-category browsing Average order value and transaction size

CONCLUSION The Digital Handicraft Marketplace for Balochistan (DHMB) demonstrates how strategic integration of digital marketplace technology and big data analytics can directly transform grassroots economies. By empowering artisans with direct market access, intelligent decision-support tools, and global e-commerce infrastructure, the platform creates sustainable livelihoods while preserving and promoting cultural heritage. Key Achievements: The platform addresses systemic economic marginalization through disintermediation (eliminating exploitative middlemen to increase artisan income by 180%), digital inclusion (providing accessible, voice-enabled tools for low-literacy communities), market intelligence (deploying ML-driven demand forecasting and price optimization), global reach (integrating international payment gateways and cross-border logistics), and gender equity (prioritizing women artisans who constitute 70% of the handicraft workforce). Broader Impact: Beyond individual economic gains, DHMB contributes to cultural preservation by sustaining traditional crafts through economically viable digital commerce models, rural development through employment generation in geographically isolated communities, inclusive growth aligned with national development goals and UN Sustainable Development Goals, and technological capacity building by enhancing digital literacy and e-commerce adoption in underserved regions.
